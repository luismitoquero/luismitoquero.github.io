My name is Luismi and I am a linguistics Phd student at the University of Southern California (USC). I am a syntactician who is mostly interested in the syntax-semantics interface. While I work primarly with Spanish data, I also do cross-linguistic research. Namely, I am concerned with the grammar of degree expressions and measurement including but not limited to comparatives, superlatives, measure phrases, number and numerals. A big part of my research focuses on how we can use syntactic diagnositics to figure out how the dimenssion of measurement is resolved, and how that information is cashed out in the syntax.

In addition, as a traditional syntactician, I am concerned with the fine-grained structure of nominals, extraction and subextraction patterns (mostly as evidenced by wh-movement) and the implications that these may have for other aspects of the theory of syntax including phasehood, agreement, concord and unlocking effects. Other projects I am interested in include cliticization and their interaction with other A/A-bar phenomena, and the syntax of comparative numerals.

Though I mostly do theoretical work, I have been (and still am) involved in some experimental research.

These are just some highlights, but I am willing to work on anything theoretically or expermentally!
